The main results establish an algorithm~\eqref{alg:sums-of-powers}
for deriving closed formulas for multifold sums of powers of integers
by combining variations of Newton's interpolation formula with
hockey-stick identities for binomial coefficients.

Using this algorithm, we obtain several notable results, including
a closed formula for sums of powers
in Proposition~\eqref{prop:ordinary-sums-of-powers-decomposition},
a formula for $r$-fold sums of powers in
Theorem~\eqref{thm:multifold-sums-of-powers},
and a pure binomial representation of $r$-fold sums of powers,
in Proposition~\eqref{prop:multifold-binomial-sums-of-powers}.

Furthermore, we show that Knuth's famous formula for sums
of odd powers~\eqref{prop:knuth-odd-powers-sum}
originates from Newton's interpolation
formula~\eqref{lem:central-newtons-formula-for-powers},
thereby obtaining its $r$-fold generalization
in Proposition~\eqref{prop:knuth-odd-powers-sum-multifold}.

Motivated by Knuth's formula for odd powers, we also employ
the centered Newton interpolation formula evaluated at zero
to derive another closed formula for sums of
powers in Proposition~\eqref{prop:ordinary-sums-of-powers-at-zero}.
This approach is extended to the $r$-fold case in
Theorem~\eqref{thm:multifold-sums-of-powers-at-zero},
and its pure binomial counterpart is established in
Proposition~\eqref{prop:multifold-sums-of-powers-at-zero-binomial-form}.
