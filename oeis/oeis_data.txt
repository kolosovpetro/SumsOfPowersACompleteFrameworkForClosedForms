Central factorial numbers of the second kind F(n,k) are defined 
by k-th central difference of power n, evaluated at zero: F(n,k) = (δ^k 0^n)/k!
Let F(t, n, k) be generalized central factorial numbers of the second kind: 
  F(t,n,k) = (δ^k t^n)/k! 
           = 1/ (2k)! * Sum_{j=0..2k} (-1)^j * binomial(2k,j) * (t+k-j)^(2n),
then:
F(0,2n,2k) = A269945(n,k) = A387597(n,k)/k!
F(1,2n,2k) = A392337(n,k)/k! = T(n,k) (this sequence);
F(2,2n,2k) = A390029(n,k)/k!

Generating function of T(2m,2k) is centered Newton's polynomial for f(n) = n^(2m): 
    n^(2m) = Sum_{k=0..2m} T(2m,k) (n-1)^[k], 
where (n-1)^[k] are central factorials.
